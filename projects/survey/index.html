01 · Advocacy & satisfaction

How likely to share with a friend?

Distribution of the 1–5 advocacy score (n=1822)

Strong advocacy. 58% gave a perfect 5 and 86% scored 4+. Only ~14% are detractors (≤3) — a healthy base for referral-led growth.

Advocacy by acquisition channel

Avg score, channels with n≥15

LinkedIn & video convert quality. LinkedIn (4.55), YouTube (4.50) and Instagram (4.46) users rate highest — social/educational channels bring the most enthusiastic users.

Audience split

Student status & sector

The audience skews graduate & academic: 71% are students, ~60% hold a Masters or PhD, and academia outweighs industry ~2.5:1. Medicine, social sciences and life sciences are the deepest verticals.
03 · Acquisition

How did you hear about us?

Normalized free-text channels

SEO is the engine. Google Search alone drives ~43% of attributed discovery. Referral ("friend") is second — consistent with the high advocacy scores. Paid (Google Ad) is small, leaving room to scale.

Competitive landscape

Other tools users mention (n=1500 mentions)

ChatGPT is the real competitor, not other research tools — it's mentioned ~4.5x more than the nearest specialist tool. Positioning should answer "why not just ChatGPT?"
04 · What users want next

Feature-request themes

Coded from 1290 open-text responses (a comment can span multiple themes)

Trust & comprehension lead the roadmap. Two themes tie at the top: citation/reference accuracy and handling figures, equations & tables — both about trusting and fully understanding the content. Summary customization, export, and in-paper Q&A follow.
05 · Trend over time

Monthly responses & advocacy

Bars = response volume · line = avg advocacy score

Watch the volume decline. Monthly responses fell from ~110/mo in early 2025 to ~15/mo in 2026, and recent advocacy dipped below 4.0 in a few months. This likely reflects a smaller, noisier sample rather than a quality collapse — worth confirming the survey is still being surfaced to new users.
